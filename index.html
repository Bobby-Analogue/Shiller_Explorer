What CAPE Yield Tells Us

Since 1881, we know the CAPE yield each month and the real total return over the following 10 years. Higher yields (cheaper markets) have historically led to better future returns. Hover over the timeline to explore what happened from any starting month.

Why CAPE Yield Works

Total return has two drivers: the return generated by the underlying business fundamentals and the change in valuation, which reflects how much investors are willing to pay for those fundamentals. Starting valuations matter because they influence the direction of valuation change. When markets begin at low valuations (high yields), the scope for a positive re-rating is greater. When they begin at high valuations (low yields), the risk of a negative de-rating increases. By contrast, returns driven by underlying fundamentals tend to be more stable over time.

When Was It This Expensive?

Every month since 1881 and its subsequent 10-year real return. Use the sliders to pick a starting valuation: matching months stay bright while the rest fade, so you can see exactly where your yield window sits in 155 years of history.

Time Tames Uncertainty

Same starting valuation, different holding periods. Over one year, outcomes vary wildly. Over longer periods the range narrows as returns are increasingly shaped by fundamentals. Time helps convert uncertainty into probability.
