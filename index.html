The Price of Exuberance

155 years of U.S. stock market valuations expressed as CAPE Yield — the real earnings power you're buying per dollar invested. When yield is low, the market is expensive, and subsequent returns have historically suffered.

Data: Prof. Robert J. Shiller, Yale University — Updated Monthly

What Yield Tells Us

Every month since 1881, we know the CAPE Yield and the subsequent 10-year annualized real total return. Higher yield means a cheaper market — and historically, better future returns. Hover over the timeline to see where any month sits.

Time Tames Uncertainty

Pick a starting valuation. At one year, the range of outcomes is enormous — anything can happen. But as the holding period lengthens, returns converge toward a predictable range. Time is the investor's best tool for managing risk.

Source: Robert J. Shiller, Irrational Exuberance, Princeton University Press.
